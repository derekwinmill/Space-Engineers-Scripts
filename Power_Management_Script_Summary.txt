### Power Management Scripting Summary

#### General Design
1. **Autonomous Power Optimization**:
   - Automatically prioritize power systems during different phases of operation (e.g., normal, defensive).

2. **Integration with Master Script**:
   - Receives alerts from the Master Script (e.g., "Under Attack") to adjust power priorities.
   - Sends updates to the Master Script about current power status (e.g., critical issues).

#### Key Features
1. **Power System Monitoring**:
   - Tracks status of batteries, reactors, solar panels, and hydrogen engines.
   - Monitors grid-wide power capacity and consumption.

2. **Defensive Prioritization**:
   - During an attack:
     - Redirects power to turrets, drones, welders, and defensive systems.
     - Reduces power allocation to non-critical systems (e.g., production facilities, interior lighting).
   - Post-combat:
     - Restores balanced power distribution.

3. **LCD Feedback**:
   - **War Room Power LCD**:
     - Displays energy reserves, current demand, and priority systems.
     - Example:
       ```
       Power Status:
       - Batteries: 85% Charged
       - Reactors: 2 Online
       - Solar: 40 MW
       - Power Distribution: Defense Priority
       ```

#### Alert Handling & Communication
- **From Master Script**:
  - Alert: "Under Attack" triggers defensive mode.
  - Alert: "Attack Over" restores normal mode.
- **To Master Script**:
  - Sends alert if grid-wide power is below a critical threshold.

#### Failsafes
1. **Emergency Overrides**:
   - Activates backup batteries or hydrogen engines if primary power sources fail.
   - Sends alerts if the base's core systems lose power.

2. **Event Logging**:
   - Logs all power switches, alerts, and critical issues to a debug LCD for troubleshooting.
